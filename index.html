The Artist

Fine Art Photography

Diana Weil works as a photographer with a background in professional advertising and industrial photography. Since 2002, she has dedicated herself to art as a freelance artist.

She uses close-up and macro photography to create colourful, meditative, and stimulating atmospheres, producing prints on Plexiglas and canvas that generate a fascinating depth.

Her works invite viewers to find peace, to discover their own essence, and to remember what is essential and true in life.

Artistic Approach

How stillness becomes depth

The Flower

Every work begins with a living flower — held at a distance of centimetres, lit with precision, and captured on analogue film. The macro lens reveals a world invisible to the naked eye: the architecture of a petal, the geometry of a stamen, colour in its most concentrated form.

The Medium

The Ice Series encases flowers in transparent blocks before photographing them. Ice refracts colour, distorts form, and creates a crystalline beauty that is both strange and deeply familiar. The Venetian Series strips the flower of all context, allowing colour and form to approach abstraction.

The Print

Works from the Venetian Series are printed directly on Plexiglas, acquiring a luminosity that no canvas can match. The Ice Series is printed on canvas over solid wood frames. Both processes are entirely analogue — each print is unique and cannot be identically reproduced.
